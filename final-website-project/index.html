Coastal Area of Grand-Bassam

Grand-Bassam is a historic town located on the southeastern coast of Ivory Coast, about 40 kilometers east of Abidjan, the country's largest city. The town was the French colonial capital of Ivory Coast from 1893 until 1900 and is now a UNESCO World Heritage site. The coastal area of Grand-Bassam is known for its beautiful beaches, lagoons, and fishing villages. The town has a population of around 84,000 people and covers an area of approximately 160 square kilometers. The climate in Grand-Bassam is tropical, with hot and humid conditions throughout the year.
